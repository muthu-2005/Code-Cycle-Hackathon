1. Types of Doping Tests

Doping tests are essential tools in maintaining the integrity of sports. The most common types of doping tests include:

  • Urine Tests: The most prevalent type of doping test, used to detect a wide range of substances.
  • Blood Tests: Used for detecting substances affecting the blood, like erythropoietin (EPO) or blood transfusions.

  • Hair and Saliva Tests: Less common but useful in specific cases for a longer detection window or non-invasive testing.

2. The Testing Process

The doping control process involves several standardized steps to ensure fair and secure results:

  1. Notification: Athletes are notified of their selection for testing by a Doping Control Officer (DCO).
  2. Sample Collection: Athletes provide a urine or blood sample under supervision.
  3. Sample Division: The sample is divided into A and B samples.
  4. Sealing and Labeling: Samples are sealed in tamper-evident containers and labeled.
  5. Documentation: Detailed documentation is maintained.
  6. Transport to Laboratory: Samples are securely transported to a WADA-accredited laboratory for analysis.
